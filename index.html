About

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Minnesota working at the intersection of machine learning and medicine. As an undergraduate research assistant at the Earl E. Bakken Medical Devices Center, I'm helping build a real-time fall detection system that uses mmWave radar instead of cameras — designing the data pipeline that turns raw sensor streams into ML-ready inputs, and contributing to infrastructure decisions across the hardware and software stack.

Having grown up across Tokyo, London, and New York, I approach healthcare technology as a global problem. The challenge that drives my work: aging populations are outpacing physician supply worldwide, with Japan as the sharpest case. I'm building toward AI diagnostic and monitoring tools for that future — and Minnesota's medical device ecosystem is where I'm learning how clinical technology actually gets built, validated, and shipped.

I work fluently in Japanese and English.
